# Music Theory Knowledge Base — Composition-Focused Overview



\## Purpose

This knowledge base is designed for **composition training**, not passive reference.

It prioritizes:

* hearing over memorizing
* writing over reading
* contrast over definitions
* short, repeatable drills
* practical transfer into a composition environment such as SunVox



\## What composers most need early

1. Rhythm and subdivision
2. Intervals and melodic contour
3. Scale degrees and tonal gravity
4. Triads, seventh chords, and chord function
5. Cadences and phrase endings
6. Motives and development
7. Form and phrase structure
8. Texture and layering
9. Ear training and active listening
10. Writing regularly and analyzing real music



\## Global teaching rules

* Teach one musical idea at a time
* Make the student hear a difference
* Use contrast drills whenever possible
* Prefer exact constraints over open-ended “experimentation”
* Move from hearing → naming → writing → varying
* Keep music theory tied to sound, expectation, and emotional effect

\## Important mindset

Theory is not a rulebook.
Theory is a map of:

* tendencies
* expectations
* relationships
* reusable patterns

A student should finish lessons with:

* something heard
* something named
* something built
* something varied



\## Skill Progression Order

Recommended learning path:

1 → Rhythm

2 → Melody

3 → Scale degrees

4 → Harmony

5 → Cadences

6 → Motives

7 → Form

8 → Texture
