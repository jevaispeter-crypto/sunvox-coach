# Counterpoint and Voice Independence

## Core idea

Counterpoint trains the ability to write multiple lines that are both:
- individually coherent
- jointly coherent

It is one of the cleanest ways to improve compositional control.

## Why it matters beyond “classical style”

Counterpoint teaches:
- line independence
- interval awareness
- melodic discipline
- dissonance control
- texture balance

## First-species value

First species is “one note against one note.”
It is intentionally strict because the student must hear:
- consonance vs dissonance
- independence vs parallelism
- line shape
- directed motion

## High-value beginner principles

- prefer stepwise motion
- leap occasionally, not constantly
- avoid overusing parallels in perfect consonances
- shape each line as a melody, not as filler
- think in horizontal motion, not only chord labels

## Core drills

### Drill 1 — two singable lines
Write a simple lower line.
Write an upper line that is also singable.

Goal:
both lines should make sense alone

### Drill 2 — consonance control
Write only consonant simultaneities.

Goal:
hear vertical smoothness

### Drill 3 — independence test
Make one line rise while the other falls.

Goal:
hear independence

### Drill 4 — avoid parallel perfects
Write two versions:
- one with repeated perfect parallels
- one without

Goal:
hear difference in independence

## Contrast prompts

Ask:
- do both lines sound like real melodies?
- do they move together too much?
- does the texture fuse into one block or stay alive?

## Common mistakes

- writing one strong line and one filler line
- thinking only vertically
- ignoring melodic quality in each part
