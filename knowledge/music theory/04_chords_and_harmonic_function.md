# Chords and Harmonic Function

## Core idea

Harmony works because chords do not feel equally stable.

In tonal music, the highest-value functional categories are:
- tonic
- predominant
- dominant

## Functional intuition

### Tonic
Feels like rest, arrival, balance, home.

### Predominant
Moves away from home and prepares dominant tension.

### Dominant
Creates directed tension that strongly wants tonic resolution.

## Why this matters

Students often learn chord names first.
What they really need is chord **behavior**.

The same chord quality means little without function in context.

## Essential beginner functions

In major:
- I = tonic
- IV / ii = predominant
- V / vii° = dominant
- vi = tonic substitute / softer prolongation

## Hearing model

I → feels stable  
IV or ii → starts moving  
V → wants to go somewhere  
I → resolves the motion

## Core drills

### Drill 1 — Function chain
Write:
I → IV → V → I

Goal:
hear departure, tension, resolution

### Drill 2 — Tonic substitute
Compare:
I → IV → V → I
with
vi → IV → V → I

Goal:
hear softer opening vs strong opening

### Drill 3 — Dominant removal
Compare:
I → IV → V → I
with
I → IV → I

Goal:
hear weaker cadence without dominant tension

### Drill 4 — Predominant contrast
Compare ii → V → I and IV → V → I.

Goal:
hear two common routes into dominant

## Contrast prompts

Ask:
- where does tension peak?
- which version feels most final?
- which opening feels softer?
- which middle chord most strongly prepares the dominant?

## Common mistakes

- labeling chords without hearing function
- assuming any major or minor chord can replace any other
- thinking V is “important” without hearing its pull to I
