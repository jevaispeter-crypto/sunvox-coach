# Ear Training and Active Listening

## Core idea

Theory that cannot be heard is weak theory.

A composer needs to hear:
- interval character
- scale-degree pull
- harmonic function
- cadence strength
- rhythmic hierarchy
- textural balance

## What to practice hearing

- step vs leap
- major vs minor third
- tonic vs dominant pull
- authentic vs open endings
- stable vs syncopated rhythm
- complete vs incomplete phrase

## Listening loop

1. hear
2. label
3. imitate
4. vary

## Core drills

### Drill 1 — Identify by contrast
Create two versions:
- resolved
- unresolved

Goal:
name which is which

### Drill 2 — Interval color
Compare:
major third vs minor third
perfect fifth vs tritone

Goal:
associate sound with quality

### Drill 3 — Function hearing
Compare:
I → V → I
and
I → IV → I

Goal:
hear stronger vs weaker tension path

### Drill 4 — Rhythmic feel
Compare:
all on-grid
vs one displaced event

Goal:
hear groove change

## Active listening prompts for songs

Ask:
- what is the pulse?
- where is the strongest arrival?
- what repeats?
- what changes?
- what creates tension?
- what resolves it?
- which layer carries the idea?

## Common mistakes

- reading theory without singing or playing it
- thinking recognition is enough without imitation
- analyzing labels without hearing function
