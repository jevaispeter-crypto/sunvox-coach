# Progressions and Cadences

\## Core idea

A progression is not just a sequence.
It is a pattern of expectation.

A cadence is where that expectation is confirmed, delayed, weakened, or denied.



\## Highest-value cadential intuitions

### Authentic cadence

Strong closure, especially if dominant clearly resolves to tonic.

### Half cadence

Stops on dominant.
Feels open.

### Plagal cadence

Gentler closure.

### Deceptive motion

Dominant avoids expected tonic arrival.
Creates surprise.



\## Why this matters

Progressions feel convincing when they manage:

* direction
* buildup
* arrival
* delayed arrival



\## Core drills

### Drill 1 — Closed vs open

Compare:
I → IV → V → I
and
I → IV → V

Goal:
hear complete vs incomplete ending

### Drill 2 — Deceptive motion

Compare:
V → I
and
V → vi

Goal:
hear expected resolution vs redirected energy

### Drill 3 — Delay arrival

Take a progression that resolves on beat 1 of the next bar.
Then move the final tonic later.

Goal:
hear stretched tension

### Drill 4 — Cadence type comparison

Write 3 short phrases:

* one authentic cadence
* one half cadence
* one deceptive ending

Goal:
hear different types of closure



\## Contrast prompts

Ask:

* which version sounds finished?
* which version sounds paused, not finished?
* which version surprises you?
* where do you feel “arrival”?



\## Common mistakes

* ending everything with full closure
* never using dominant preparation
* calling a weak ending “resolved” without hearing it



\## Retrieval tags

progression, cadence, authentic cadence, half cadence, deceptive, closure



\## Evaluation check



A strong cadence:

\- creates tension before resolution

\- clearly changes energy at the end
