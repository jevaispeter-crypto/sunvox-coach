# 06\. Reverb, Delay, and Depth

## Core idea

Space effects do not only add atmosphere.
They also change:

* depth
* separation
* movement
* apparent size

Used well, they help organize the mix.
Used poorly, they blur it.



## What the student must understand

* dry vs wet
* reverb
* delay
* early reflections
* decay
* pre-delay
* depth
* clutter



## Why it matters

Weak production often comes from:

* putting reverb on everything
* adding space before balance is stable
* confusing “big” with “far away”
* using long tails that mask rhythm and articulation

Strong production usually has:

* intentional depth hierarchy
* selective use of ambience
* enough space to create dimension without losing clarity



## Hearing model

### Reverb

Reverb simulates a space around the sound.

More reverb often makes a sound feel:

* further away
* softer in attack
* less dry / direct



### Delay

Delay repeats the sound after a defined time.

Unlike reverb, delay can preserve more rhythmic identity.



### Depth

Dry and forward:

* close
* direct

Wet and diffuse:

* further back
* less immediate



\### Depth hierarchy (practical)



Think in layers:



\- Front → dry, loud, clear attack

\- Mid → moderate reverb or delay

\- Back → more reverb, softer attack



Not every sound should be equally present.

Depth creates separation without EQ.



### Pre-delay

Pre-delay separates the dry attack from the reverb bloom.

This can preserve clarity while still adding space.



## Production heuristics

* Build dry balance first
* Use less reverb than you think
* Use delay when you want space without losing as much articulation
* Treat depth as hierarchy: not every sound should sit in the same plane
* If the mix turns blurry, reduce tails before doing more EQ



## Core drills

### Drill 1 — Dry vs wet position

Compare a dry element and a reverberant version.

Goal:
hear forwardness vs distance



### Drill 2 — Delay vs reverb

Use a short delay and then a reverb on the same sound.

Goal:
hear rhythmic clarity vs diffusion



### Drill 3 — Pre-delay awareness

Add reverb with and without pre-delay.

Goal:
hear how attack clarity changes



### Drill 4 — Depth hierarchy

Place one element dry, one moderately wet, one clearly further back.

Goal:
hear front-to-back arrangement



## Contrast prompts

Ask:

* does this sound need space or just better balance?
* is this bigger, or only blurrier?
* which effect preserves rhythm better?
* does the main element still feel present?



## Common mistakes

* washing everything in reverb
* using long tails on already dense material
* adding ambience before solving tonal / balance problems
* collapsing the depth hierarchy by giving everything similar space
* forgetting that silence and dryness are also spatial choices



## Retrieval tags

## In SunVox

* add space after raw balance is stable
* use short delays when you want motion with clearer rhythm
* use reverb to place sounds behind or around the focal point
